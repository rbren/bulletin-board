# Cambridge & Somerville News Bulletin
*Updated: January 10, 2026*

## Somerville

### Politics & Government
**Jake Wilson Inaugurated as 37th Mayor**  
*January 5, 2026*

Jake Wilson was sworn in as Somerville's new mayor, succeeding Katjana Ballantyne. The two-term city councilor is bringing his "urban mechanic" philosophy to City Hall, focusing on improving basic city services with measurable results. Wilson, who has lived in Winter Hill since 2004, promises better response times, enhanced communication, and transparent tracking of city projects. His immediate priorities include diversifying housing types, strengthening tenant protections, and improving 311 response times.

### Sports & Recreation
**Nonprofit Semi-Pro Soccer Team Launches This Spring**  
*January 6, 2026*

Somerville United FC will begin its inaugural season in spring 2026 as part of the American Premier Soccer League. Founded by Robert Sifa Onjiko and Alesandra Grace Onjiko, the nonprofit team will play home games at Dilboy Memorial Stadium (110 Alewife Brook Parkway). Tryouts begin in early February. The founders plan to launch a women's team later in 2026, which would be the first of its kind in the region.

### Business & Economy
**Tessera Therapeutics Announces Major Layoffs**  
*January 9, 2026*

The genome engineering company headquartered at 101 South St. will lay off 90 employees effective March 8, 2026. The layoffs represent a significant portion of the workforce as the company refocuses on bringing two priority treatments into clinical trials. Tessera specializes in gene sequencing and writing technology aimed at curing autoimmune diseases like sickle cell.

**K-Bok Korean Fried Chicken Opens in Union Square**  
*Now Open*

A new Korean fried chicken restaurant has opened at 249 Washington St. in Union Square. K-Bok specializes in boneless Korean fried chicken with signature flavors including soy garlic, spicy yangnyeom, and original crispy wings. The menu also features a double-fried chicken sandwich on brioche with coleslaw and pickles.

## Cambridge

### Restaurants & Dining
**Gato Exotico Brings Mexican Flavors to CambridgeSide**  
*Now Open*

A new Mexican restaurant and bar has opened at 100 Cambridgeside Place. From the team behind Wusong Road, Gato Exotico features an extensive taco menu ($5-$6), large plates including birria short rib and carne asada, and Cambridge's widest selection of tequilas and agave spirits. The vibrant space embraces a fiesta atmosphere with tropical decor and creative tiki-style cocktails.

### Public Safety
**Fatal Stabbing on Norfolk Street**  
*January 7-8, 2026*

Cambridge Police arrested Victor Rivas, 51, of South Boston on charges of assault and battery with a dangerous weapon after a fatal stabbing at a residential building on Norfolk Street on January 7. The victim died at a local hospital. Police say the suspect and victim knew each other and the incident was isolated with no broader public safety threat.

### Infrastructure
**Memorial Drive Redesign Under Discussion**  
*Ongoing*

The city is considering redesign options for Memorial Drive that could reshape traffic patterns along Cambridge's Charles River waterfront. Community input sessions are being held to gather feedback on the proposals.

### Arts & Culture
**New Civil Rights Play Debuts in Cambridge**  
*January 2026*

A new stage production exploring the relationship between Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. and Stokely Carmichael is being performed in Cambridge. The play offers an intimate look at the human side of the Civil Rights Movement through imagined conversations between the two leaders.
