## Trump Orders Military Strikes on ISIS Targets in Syria

President Trump authorized retaliatory strikes against Islamic State group positions in Syria on Saturday as part of ongoing operations against ISIS. US Central Command announced the "large-scale strikes" as part of Operation Hawkeye Strike.

## Trump Intensifies Push for US Control of Greenland

Trump stated the US will take control of Greenland "the easy way" or "the hard way," arguing it's necessary to prevent Russia and China from acquiring the Danish territory. Denmark has reiterated that Greenland is not for sale. The White House press secretary indicated military force remains "always an option."

## Fatal ICE Shooting in Minneapolis Triggers Protests and Controversy

The death of Renee Nicole Good in a shooting involving ICE agents in Minneapolis has sparked widespread protests and raised questions about ICE's use of deadly force. Multiple demonstrations have taken place in Minneapolis and other cities. Separately, Rep. Ilhan Omar and two other House members were blocked from visiting an ICE facility in Minnesota. The FBI has taken over the investigation, with local leaders expressing concerns about fairness.

## Trump Pursues Venezuela Oil Infrastructure Investment

Trump met with oil and gas executives to discuss plans for US investment in Venezuela's oil infrastructure, seeking approximately $100 billion. The president canceled a planned "second wave" of attacks on Venezuela, citing the country's cooperation. Venezuela has released imprisoned opposition figures as requested by the US. However, oil executives have expressed skepticism, with one calling Venezuela "uninvestible."

## Federal Judge Blocks Trump Administration's Childcare Funding Freeze

A federal judge temporarily halted the Trump administration's freeze on childcare subsidies and social assistance payments to Democratic-led states. The administration had suspended approximately $129 million in benefits to states including Minnesota, New York, California, Colorado, and others without providing clear justification for targeting specific states.

## Trump Proposes 10% Cap on Credit Card Interest Rates

Trump announced a proposal for a one-year, 10% cap on credit card interest rates. The American Bankers Association criticized the proposal, warning it would "drive consumers toward less regulated, more costly alternatives."

## Judge Blocks Trump Election Order in Vote-by-Mail Lawsuit

A federal judge blocked Trump's executive order related to elections in a lawsuit filed by Oregon and Washington, both vote-by-mail states. The ruling temporarily prevents implementation of the order while the case proceeds.

**Sources:**
- [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/10/politics/judge-blocks-trump-election-vote-mail-oregon-washington)

## Trump Warns Iran Over Violent Crackdown on Protesters

Following violent suppression of protests in Iran, Trump warned the regime "we'll start shooting" if more protesters are killed. Iran's Prosecutor General has threatened protesters with charges of being "an enemy of God," which carries the death penalty in the Islamic Republic.
